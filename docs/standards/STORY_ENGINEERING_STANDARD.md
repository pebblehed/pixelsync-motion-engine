# Story Engineering Standard

---

# 1. Purpose

This standard defines the engineering principles governing narrative construction within the PixelSync Motion Engine.

Story Engineering is responsible for transforming information into understanding through structured cinematic communication.

Every Production created using the Motion Engine begins with Story Engineering.

---

# 2. Responsibility

Story Engineering defines:

- Why the Production exists.
- Who the intended audience is.
- What understanding should be achieved.
- The desired audience outcome.
- The emotional progression.
- The narrative progression.

Story Engineering intentionally does not define:

- Visual implementation.
- Camera behaviour.
- Motion behaviour.
- Typography.
- Lighting.
- Audio.
- Runtime behaviour.
- Rendering.

These responsibilities belong to their respective Engineering Standards.

---

# 3. Engineering Objectives

Every Story shall:

- Communicate clearly.
- Progress logically.
- Build understanding.
- Reduce cognitive effort.
- Reinforce confidence.
- Support the intended audience outcome.

Story shall always communicate with clarity before spectacle.

---

# 4. Story Structure

Every Production shall contain a defined narrative progression.

Story Engineering requires logical progression of understanding but does **not** prescribe a single storytelling framework.

The Motion Engine supports multiple narrative structures provided they satisfy the engineering objectives defined within this standard.

One example progression is:

```text
Recognition
        ↓
Problem
        ↓
Understanding
        ↓
Solution
        ↓
Confidence
        ↓
Call To Action
```

Alternative narrative frameworks may be adopted where appropriate, provided they maintain logical communication and audience understanding.

---

# 5. Story Components

A Story is composed from reusable narrative elements.

Typical Story components may include:

- Opening Hook
- Context
- Problem
- Investigation
- Insight
- Solution
- Validation
- Outcome
- Closing Message

The exact composition of a Story shall be determined by the communication objective.

Additional Story components may be introduced where justified.

---

# 6. Story Quality Principles

High-quality Stories demonstrate:

- Clear purpose.
- Logical sequencing.
- Progressive understanding.
- Emotional consistency.
- Appropriate pacing.
- Audience relevance.

Story complexity shall never exceed the audience's ability to understand.

---

# 7. Relationship to Other Standards

Story Engineering provides direction for:

- Scene Engineering
- Sequence Engineering
- Component Engineering
- Behaviour Engineering

These Engineering Standards inherit the narrative intent established by Story Engineering.

Specialist Engineering Standards, including Motion, Camera, Typography, Lighting and Audio Engineering, ultimately inherit this narrative direction through the established architectural hierarchy.

---

# 8. Validation

A Story Engineering implementation shall satisfy the following conditions.

✓ Defines a communication objective.

✓ Identifies the intended audience.

✓ Defines the desired audience outcome.

✓ Provides logical narrative progression.

✓ Can be decomposed into one or more Scenes.

✓ Supports audience understanding.
